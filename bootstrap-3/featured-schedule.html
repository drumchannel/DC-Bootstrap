 Featured Lessons

Studio Tips

Instructor: Curt Bisquera

In this lesson Curt will share what he feels is most important when you go into the studio for the first time. Curt is a first-call touring and session drummer and as a seasoned veteran of the industry he has plenty of knowledge to offer.

Incorporating Percussion

Instructor: Walfredo Reyes Jr.

Walfredo, percussionist from the band Chicago, will demonstrate how you can begin to add percussion into your drum set playing and add more depth and dimension to your sound.

Rim Shot Technique

Instructor: Chad Smith

This is an answer to a question in DC Feedback about playing rim shots. It is a basic idea but watch closely how relaxed Chad is at the point of impact. That's the real lesson. Also his secret tip about hitting between the lugs - a real secret from a pro.

12-Bar Blues Solo

Instructor: Gregg Bisonette

Gregg Bissonette is one of the most versatile drummers in the music industry. He has played with artists such as David Lee Roth, Ringo Starr, and Maynard Ferguson. Gregg shows us some tips and soloing ideas for a 12 bar blues phrase.

25 or 6 to 4

Instructor: Danny Seraphine

Pioneering jazz-rock drummer and composer Danny Seraphine is a founding member and drummer of the iconic band Chicago which is being inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame this week!

Fills from Nate Morton (The Voice)

Instructor: Nate Morton

Nate Morton is the drummer for NBC's program "The Voice". Nate gives an example of how you can take a basic concept or pattern, and maximize its effectiveness through creativity and voicing.

Syncopation for the Set

Instructor: Rich Redmond

Rich Redmond is an in demand country and rock drummer, playing with Jason Aldean, Kelly Clarkson, Bryan Adams, and Jewel to name a few. He has played on 17 #1 singles and has sold over 7 million albums and 13 million single downloads.

Serious Shuffles

Instructor: Rick Latham

Rick Latham has played with some of the biggest names in the music industry. In this series of lessons, he will show you different stylistic interpretations of the shuffle, as well as exercises to improve your groove and coordination as it relates to the shuffle.
