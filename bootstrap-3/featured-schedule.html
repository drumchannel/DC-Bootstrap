Dave Elitch's

Paradiddlediddle Perfection

Master Class

Dave Elitch has played with The Mars Volta, Miley Cyrus, and many others. He is known in the music industry as a highly versatile and musical drummer. In this series of lessons, he will show you how to maximize your use of the paradiddlediddle rudiment. You will learn how to play it properly, and then how to use it in different scenarios with different levels of technicality and independence.

 Featured Lessons

Fills Around the Kit

Instructor: John Robinson

JR Robinson is the most recorded drummer in history. He has played with legends like Michael Jackson, Chaka Khan, and Quincy Jones. With his impecable time, JR has gotten his name on hundreds of hit records

The Art of Soloing

Instructor: Paul Wertico

Paul Wertico gained worldwide recognition as a member of the Pat Metheny Group from 1983 to 2001. Paul will give you some insight as to how you can shape your soloing sound and individual voice with some of the choices you make on the drums.

Morello's Hand Technique

Instructor: Joe Morello

Joe Morello is well known for his drumming in The Dave Brubeck Quartet. He has been on over 120 albums and is most popular for his work on "Take Five". Joe Morello discusses how he holds the stick and uses fingers and wrist turns.

Authentic latin Rhythms

Instructor: Alex Acuña

Alex Acuña is both a master and historian of Latin rhythms. He covers Cha Cha Cha, Bolero, Salsa, Son Montuno, Caribbean Rhythms, African Rhythms, Samba, Rumba, Tango, Meringue and Mambo.

29 - Inverted Flam Tap

Instructor: Adam Kuns

This is the archetypical inverted rudiment. It combines a three stroke motion on each hand but you pull the accent out on the last note of the three.

Tuesday: 3/29

30 - Flam Drags

Instructor: Adam Kuns

This rudiment could fit within the flam rudiments or the drag rudiments. If you have trouble with this one at first, don't worry! Come back to it after completing the Tap Drag.

Master Class

Wednesday: 3/30

31 - Drag

Instructor: Adam Kuns

Time to learn our fundamentals! The drag is a simple concept and how well you execute it sets the difficulty for all of the upcoming rudiments.

Master Class

Thursday: 3/31

32 - Tap Drag

Instructor: Adam Kuns

The tap drag is a pattern that can be used as a "check" for the Flam drag. Try to keep your hands relaxed and moving evenly in tempo.
