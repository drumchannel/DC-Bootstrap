Indian Rhythms

Instructor: Pete Lockett

Pete Lockett systematically demonstrates the fundamentals of the Indian musical rhythmic structures. He demonstrates techniques on the Tablas, Kanjira, Darbuka, Cajon and drum set. He applies these techniques in both traditional and contemporary musical settings.

Difficulty: Advanced  12 Lessons

Pete Lockett - Indian Rhythms and Percussion


 

Northern Indian Rhythms - Part 1

Pete will show you the basic fundamentals of Northern Indian rhythms and how they differ from western ideas and structure.


 

Northern Indian Rhythms - Part 2

Pete expands upon the ideas presented in the previous lesson on Northern Indian rhythms and demonstrates some other ideas and how they can be tied to Western music.


 

Southern Indian Rhythms - Part 1

Pete will show you the basic fundamentals of Southern Indian rhythms and how they differ from western ideas and structure.


 

Southern Indian Rhythms - Part 2

Pete expands upon the ideas presented in the previous lesson on Southern Indian rhythms and demonstrates some other ideas and how they can be tied to Western music.


 

Cajon Playing

Pete demonstrates some of the fundamental aspects of playing cajon. This includes exercises and techniques that you can use to practice getting a proper sound on the instrument.


 

Kanjira Solo - Lucky 13

Pete demonstrates the playing a traditional Indian piece of music using a Kanjira following the style and structure of the genre.


 

Korvai 63

Pete performs a song playing the cajon featuring ancient South Indian rhythms.


 

James Brown Tablas

This is a cool example of how you can integrate the traditional tabla drums into a western music scenario. Pete plays along to a James Brown tune entirely on the Tabla.


 

Traditional Tablas

This is a much more traditional example of tabla playing in its original environment. Notice the differences between this and the James brown example.


 

Tablas and Darbuka

Pete plays both the tablas and the darbuka and you can hear the difference between the two. Both sonically and the way they are used rhythmically.


 

Treketay

Now we can put it all together and apply the principles presented to us regarding Indian rhythms and apply them in a traditional setting on the drum set. This features Fazal Qureshi on drums.


 

Rivers of Time

This last example demonstrates a very modern approach to using these rhythms and principles. This features traditional carnatic vocal compositions.
